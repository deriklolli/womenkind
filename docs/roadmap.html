Phase 1

Foundation

B–01
Patient Intake ✓ shipped

We built a 54-question clinical intake spanning 10 health domains, with adaptive branching that shows or hides questions based on prior answers. Patients move through sections covering symptoms, history, medications, and lifestyle before signing a consent gate that unlocks the rest of the platform.

B–02
AI Clinical Brief ✓ shipped

The moment a patient submits their intake, Claude synthesizes their answers into a structured clinical brief that is waiting for Dr. Urban before the visit begins. The brief is organized into four tabs covering symptom summary, health history, treatment considerations, and recommended talking points, all grounded in IMS 2024 menopause guidelines.

B–03
Provider Queue & Auth ✓ shipped

We stood up the full authentication system, connecting Supabase identity to a separate clinical database so patient records never touch the auth layer. Dr. Urban logs in and sees a queue of patients sorted by intake submission date, with each row gated until the AI brief has finished generating.

B–04
Stripe Billing ✓ shipped

Full payment infrastructure went live with six products covering the $650 intake visit and three tiers of the $200 per month membership. We activated a FOUNDING member promo code and wired Stripe checkout directly into the onboarding flow so payment happens before the first appointment is booked.

B–05
Onboarding Funnel ✓ shipped

A patient arriving for the first time moves through a linear funnel: welcome screen, plan selection, account creation, email verification, intake, and payment. Verification links use cryptographic tokens so a patient who clicks the link on a different device than they signed up on still lands in the right place without errors.

Phase 2

Patient Experience

B–06
Patient Dashboard ✓ shipped

The patient-facing dashboard went live with a full suite of health tracking cards, one for each of the ten clinical domains. The experience unlocks immediately after a patient submits their intake, and each domain card shows a live sparkline drawn from their real check-in history so progress is visible from the first visit.

B–07
WMI Scoring Engine ✓ shipped

We built the WomenKind Menopause Index, a proprietary scoring algorithm that converts a patient's intake answers into a single numeric score across ten health domains. The score updates dynamically as patients log weekly check-ins, and when an Oura ring is connected, sleep and energy readings flow in automatically without requiring manual input.

B–08
Daily Check-in & Wearables ✓ shipped

Patients can log how they are feeling each day with a short check-in that adapts to their health profile. Questions are tailored per domain, so vasomotor symptoms are tracked by episode count, sleep by hours, and mood on a wellness scale. When a patient wears an Oura ring, sleep and energy questions are skipped entirely since the wearable is already capturing that data.

B–09
Pillar Trend Chart ✓ shipped

A custom 24-week trend chart shows each patient how their health has changed over time across all ten domains. The chart overlays milestone pins on the timeline marking key moments like the initial consultation and any prescription changes, so patients can visually connect their treatment decisions to shifts in how they feel.

B–10
Patient Engagement System ✓ shipped

We built a proactive outreach system to keep patients engaged between visits. It sends a weekly nudge if no check-in has been logged, a monthly progress recap, and an alert to the care team if a patient's score drops sharply. Every email includes a one-click unsubscribe and all outreach is subject to frequency caps so patients never feel overwhelmed.

Phase 3

Clinical Operations

B–11
Video Visits ✓ shipped

Patients can book appointments directly from their dashboard and join video visits through a HIPAA-compliant video platform. Calls are recorded automatically in the cloud with encryption and access controls required by healthcare law, and recordings are stored in a dedicated secure bucket tied to the patient record.

B–12
Recording & Transcription Pipeline ✓ shipped

After every visit, the recording is automatically transcribed and passed to Claude, which generates a structured clinical SOAP note and saves it to the patient record. Providers can also capture ambient audio during in-person encounters, which goes through the same transcription pipeline and produces a note without any manual documentation.

B–13
Prescriptions & Lab Orders ✓ shipped

Dr. Urban can write prescriptions and order labs directly from a patient's profile. When lab results come in, they are automatically surfaced on the patient record and the patient receives a notification. Both prescription history and lab orders are preserved in the clinical record for reference at every future visit.

B–14
Dashboard Polish & Notification Prefs ✓ shipped

We refined the patient dashboard with richer visual feedback, including color-tinted average pills on each domain card, trend sparklines drawn from real check-in data, and a score card showing the numeric change since the last measurement. Patients also gained control over which notifications they receive, with granular preferences across three categories.

B–15
Signup & Membership Fixes ✓ shipped

We resolved a series of reliability issues that were affecting new patient signups, including email verification links failing to deliver and membership status not being recognized consistently across the app. The billing system moved fully into live mode with real payment processing, and the checkout experience was redesigned to make plan selection clearer before a patient creates an account.

Phase 4

Practice OS

B–16
Multi-Staff Clinical Command Center 3–4 weeks

The platform evolves from a single-physician tool into a full clinical operating system supporting six staff roles: MD, NP, RN, MA, admin, and concierge. Every patient action that requires follow-up generates a task routed to the right person, with a strict status workflow that ensures nothing falls through the cracks. Separate queue views for RN and admin staff mean each role sees only what is relevant to them.

B–17
Patient Cockpit 2–3 weeks

Dr. Urban gets a single-screen view of everything relevant about a patient before touching their chart: current treatment, recent symptom changes, open tasks, and what has shifted since the last review. Any time a medication is changed, the system automatically schedules a five-point follow-up cadence at day four, week four, week eight, week twelve, and annually, so no patient on a new protocol goes unmonitored.

B–18
AI Clinical Intelligence 3–4 weeks

Claude is brought deeper into the clinical workflow as an assistant, not a decision-maker. It generates a one-line patient summary, highlights what has changed since the last MD review, and drafts message responses for staff to edit and send. All AI suggestions are explicitly labeled and require human approval before any action is taken. Clinical recommendations come from physician-authored protocol checklists, never from AI-generated reasoning.

B–19
Service Recovery Dashboard ~2 weeks

A dedicated dashboard gives the admin team visibility into any patient who has raised a concern, expressed dissatisfaction, or has an open billing dispute. Each issue is tracked through to resolution with a full log of actions taken, ensuring that no complaint sits unaddressed and the practice can identify patterns over time.

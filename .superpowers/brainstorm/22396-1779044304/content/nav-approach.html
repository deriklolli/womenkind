How should the multi-role portal navigate?

Three approaches for how MD, RN, admin, and concierge land in the app

A

Role-Aware Single Portal

One URL (/provider/dashboard). Nav links change based on your staff role. MD sees "Today" + patients + schedule. RN sees "RN Queue" + patients. Concierge sees "Admin Queue" + messages. Same shell, different links and default landing page per role.

Pros

  • Familiar — same login, same shell
  • Less routing complexity
  • Easier to build "patient profile" as shared surface

Cons

  • Nav logic has role conditionals everywhere
B

Separate Staff Sub-Portal

MD stays at /provider/*. RN, admin, concierge get a new /staff/* route. Different layouts, separate nav, designed for task-queue-first workflows vs. patient-chart-first workflows.

Pros

  • Clean separation — staff UI is task-queue-native
  • Easier to design for mobile (RN on iPad)

Cons

  • More routes to build and maintain
  • Patient profile needs to work in both shells
C

Unified Portal, Role-Gated Pages

All staff share /provider/* routes. Each page has a required role. MD tries to hit /staff/rn-queue → redirected to their Today page. RN tries to hit /provider/today → redirected to RN queue. Nav always shows all links, grays out inaccessible ones.

Pros

  • Simple routing
  • Staff can see what other roles do (transparency)

Cons

  • Grayed links feel like a downgrade experience
  • Less tailored per role

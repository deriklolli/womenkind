Why these numbers.

Three pricing decisions, each grounded in a specific competitor signal.

$650 intake stays. It's higher than Uplift ($435) and Heron ($385) but dramatically less than Cenegenics ($4,495+). It justifies the 1–2 hour MD consultation and the personalized care presentation — and it's consistent with where your existing patients enter today. No reason to change it.

$249 Vitality replaces $200/mo. A $49 monthly increase in exchange for an annual lab panel, formal Oura clinical integration, and a clearer service definition. Existing patients get the upgraded scope; new patients see a Park City-priced tier that still undercuts Cenegenics' $550 floor by half. Annual prepay at $2,490 ($208/mo effective) gives committed patients a meaningful discount.

$549 Concierge defines the ceiling. Priced just under Cenegenics' lowest monthly fee, this tier captures the high-net-worth Park City buyer who wants depth without buying into longevity-tech maximalism. Most members won't choose it — but its existence makes Vitality look like the obvious value.

Lab pricing & insurance posture. Womenkind operates as an out-of-network cash-pay practice. The annual panel included in Vitality and the semi-annual panels in Concierge run through our negotiated Labcorp/Quest cash rates — typically 40–60% below what insurance would be billed. Members pay no additional cost for the included panels; reactive labs are at our negotiated cash rate. Superbills are available for members who wish to submit to their insurance for possible reimbursement.

"The strongest pricing argument we can make in Park City is honesty: we're not the cheapest hormone clinic and we're not Cenegenics. We're the women's specialty clinic in between — and that's where most of our patients want to be."

07

What I need from you.

Three open questions before we move this from proposal to live pricing on the website.

1. Visit time math. Does the cadence (Foundations 15-min quarterly · Vitality 30-min quarterly · Concierge 60-min monthly) match what you can sustainably deliver as patient volume grows? If we hit 50 Vitality members, that's ~50 30-min visits per quarter — call it 25 hours of MD time. Workable, or do we need to plan for an NP earlier?

2. Annual lab panel scope for Vitality. What gets included at the $249 tier — CBC, CMP, lipid, full thyroid, hormone panel, A1c, vitamin D? And what stays as an add-on (e.g., reverse T3, ApoB)? I'd like the included panel defined precisely so we don't have scope creep.

3. Existing patient transition. Current $200/mo members move to Vitality at $249. I'd recommend grandfathering them at $200 for 6 months while we communicate the expanded scope (annual labs, Oura clinical integration, cleaner messaging response SLA), then move them to the new rate at renewal. Open to your preferred handling.

4. Lab panel scope, locked. The Vitality panel as written includes CBC, CMP, lipid, A1c, full thyroid (TSH, free T3, free T4), hormone panel (estradiol, FSH, LH, progesterone, total + free testosterone, SHBG, DHEA-S), and vitamin D. Cost-of-goods at our negotiated Labcorp rate is approximately $150–$200 per member per year. Confirm this is the right scope, and flag anything you'd add (reverse T3, ApoB, ferritin) or move to add-on pricing.
